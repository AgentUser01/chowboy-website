Thanksgiving Leftover Casserole

"Turkey, mashed potatoes, cheese, a special sauce, and some little extras combine to make a tasty casserole for any day of the week. Absolutely delicious way to use those tasty Thanksgiving leftovers." Original recipe yields 8 servings.

Prep Time

30 min

Cook Time

45 min

Total Time

75 min

Servings

8

Difficulty

Medium

Ingredients

  • •3.0 tablespoons butter
  • •2.0 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • •0.5 cup evaporated milk
  • •1.0 cup water
  • •0.2 teaspoon salt
  • •0.2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • •0.8 teaspoon onion powder
  • •2.0 tablespoons butter
  • •1.0 cup fine dry herb-seasoned bread stuffing mix
  • •1.0 cup cooked, diced turkey meat
  • •1.0 cup shredded Cheddar cheese
  • •2.0 cups leftover mashed potatoes

Nutrition (per serving)

Calories366

Instructions

  1. 1

    Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Lightly grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.

  2. 2

    Melt 3 tablespoons butter in a saucepan over low heat. Blend in the flour; cook for 5 minutes, or until bubbling. Slowly stir in evaporated milk and water, then season with salt, pepper, and onion powder. Stir sauce over low heat for 5 minutes.

  3. 3

    In a separate saucepan over low heat, melt 2 tablespoons butter. Stir in the dry stuffing mix. Place the turkey in the prepared baking dish. Pour the sauce over turkey, then sprinkle with Cheddar cheese. Spread mashed potatoes over cheese. Top mashed potatoes with the stuffing mixture.

  4. 4

    Bake 45 minutes in the preheated oven.
