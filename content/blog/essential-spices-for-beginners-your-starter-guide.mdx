# What Spices Should I Buy First? A Starter Guide

Welcome to the flavorful world of spices! If you've recently taken to the kitchen or are looking to refine your culinary skills, you might find yourself staring at the endless rows of spices in the grocery store, wondering, "What spices should I buy first?" Fear not, dear reader, as we embark on a spice-filled journey that will not only answer this burning question but also transform your cooking from ordinary to extraordinary.

## The Essential Spice Starter Kit

Embarking on your spice journey doesn't require an overflowing cabinet from the get-go. Starting with a well-rounded selection can elevate your cooking, allowing you to experiment while keeping your dishes grounded in familiar flavors. Here's a list of the must-have spices for any kitchen:

### 1. Salt
- **Type:** Sea salt or Himalayan pink salt
- **Why:** Enhances the natural flavors of your dish
- **Use:** From pasta water to seasoning meats

### 2. Black Pepper
- **Type:** Freshly ground for peak flavor
- **Why:** Adds a mild heat and depth
- **Use:** Virtually everything can benefit from a pinch of black pepper

### 3. Garlic Powder
- **Type:** Fine ground
- **Why:** Delivers a savory depth without the hassle of peeling and chopping
- **Use:** Soups, marinades, meats, and vegetables

### 4. Paprika
- **Type:** Sweet, Smoked, or Hot
- **Why:** Imparts color and a smoky note
- **Use:** Rubs, soups, and as a garnish for a vibrant splash of color

### 5. Cumin
- **Type:** Whole seeds or ground
- **Why:** Earthy and aromatic, it’s essential for spice blends
- **Use:** Curries, taco seasoning, and Middle Eastern dishes

### 6. Cinnamon
- **Type:** Sticks or ground
- **Why:** Warm and sweet, perfect for desserts and savory dishes
- **Use:** Baked goods, curries, and even coffee

### 7. Red Pepper Flakes
- **Type:** Crushed
- **Why:** Adds a spicy kick
- **Use:** Pizza, pasta sauces, and to infuse oils

### 8. Oregano
- **Type:** Dried
- **Why:** Minty and slightly bitter, it’s a staple in Italian and Greek cuisine
- **Use:** Tomato-based dishes, marinades, and dressings

Creating your spice starter kit with these essentials ensures you’re prepared to tackle a diverse range of recipes. ## Practical Tips for Storing and Using Spices

### Maximizing Freshness
- **Store in a cool, dark place:** Heat, light, and moisture can diminish the potency of spices.
- **Buy in small quantities:** Freshness is key, so buy smaller amounts more frequently.

### Experimenting with Flavors
- **Start with less:** You can always add more, but you can’t take away. Begin with a modest amount and adjust to taste.
- **Toast whole spices:** Toasting in a dry pan can enhance their flavor before grinding.

## Cooking with Spices: Real Examples and Measurements

### Example 1: Classic Tomato Sauce
- **Ingredients:** 1 tsp oregano, ½ tsp garlic powder, ½ tsp salt, ¼ tsp black pepper
- **Method:** Simmer these spices with canned tomatoes for a quick and flavorful sauce.

### Example 2: Simple Spice-Rubbed Chicken
- **Ingredients:** 1 tsp paprika, ½ tsp garlic powder, ½ tsp salt, ¼ tsp black pepper, ¼ tsp cumin
- **Method:** Mix the spices and rub onto chicken breasts before cooking to add a burst of flavor.

Remember, these measurements are starting points. Adjust according to your taste preferences and the size of the dish you’re preparing.
